Random forest
Random forests are low variance models due to averaging.
Predictions remain stable when data slightly changes.
This property makes them common for production pipelines.

Random forest
Random forests cannot extrapolate outside training range.
Prediction becomes the average value in the nearest region.
Example:
Training data:
$$
x \in [0,10]
$$
Prediction at:
$$
x=20
$$
Forest usually returns values close to the maximum training region.

Concept:
Random forests approximate the function through local partitioning of feature space.

Random forest
Function approximated as piecewise regions.
Example conceptual representation:
$$
f(x) = \begin{cases} 3.2 & x_1 < 1.2 \ 4.7 & x_1 \ge 1.2 \end{cases}
$$
Forest smooths these discontinuities through averaging.

Random forest
Random forests follow a deterministic training procedure:
Draw bootstrap samples.
Grow decision trees.
At each split choose a random subset of features.
Objective per split:
$$
\min \text{MSE}
$$
No search over mathematical forms occurs.
